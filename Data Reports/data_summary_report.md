# Tennessee Foster Care Youth Data Summary Report

## Executive Summary

This report summarizes data from the Tennessee foster care youth dataset, focusing on three key areas of focus: **Housing Stability**, **Mental Health**, and **Supportive Adult Relationships**. The analysis is based on data from the `/data/` folder, including youth demographic information and CANS (Child and Adolescent Needs and Strengths) assessment data.

## Dataset Overview

### Data Sources
- **df_youth.csv**: Contains 1,999 youth records with demographic and placement information
- **df_assessment_questions.csv**: Contains CANS assessment data with detailed scoring for various domains
- **IL YOUTH DATASET FROM FY23-25.xlsx**: Additional youth dataset (33MB)

### Key Demographics
- **Total Youth**: 1,999 individuals
- **Age Range**: Primarily 17-21 years old (transitioning youth)
- **Gender Distribution**: Mixed male and female representation
- **Geographic Coverage**: Multiple counties across Tennessee

## 3. Supportive Adult Relationships

### Assessment Indicators
The CANS assessment includes several indicators related to supportive adult relationships:

#### Family Functioning
- **Assessment Type**: CANS
- **Description**: Evaluates relationships with family members
- **Scoring Scale**: 0-3 (0 = No problems, 3 = Severe problems requiring immediate intervention)

**Key Findings:**
- History or suspicion of problems in family relationships
- Some family members may have problems in their relationships with youth
- Arguing may be common but does not result in major problems

#### Natural Supports
- **Assessment Type**: CANS
- **Description**: Evaluates youth's natural support systems outside of family and paid caregivers
- **Scoring Scale**: 0-3 (0 = Well-developed strength, 3 = No current strength identified)

**Key Findings:**
- Some identified natural supports but require significant strength building efforts
- Natural supports not actively contributing to youth's healthy development
- Need for efforts to identify potential strengths

#### Family Strengths
- **Assessment Type**: CANS
- **Description**: Evaluates family's ability to provide emotional and concrete support
- **Scoring Scale**: 0-3 (0 = Well-developed strength, 3 = No current strength identified)

**Key Findings:**
- Some good relationships and communication within families
- At least one family member with strong, loving relationship with youth
- Ability to provide limited emotional or concrete support

#### Additional Relationship Indicators
- **Relationship Permanence**: Assessment of stability in relationships over time
- **Attachment Difficulties**: Evaluation of caregiver-youth relationship quality
- **Disruptions In Caregiving/Attachment Losses**: Assessment of placement changes and their impact

### County Distribution for Supportive Adult Relationship Issues

## Recommendations

### For Housing Stability
1. **Placement Stability**: Focus on reducing placement changes and improving living situation functioning
2. **Geographic Consistency**: Minimize geographic moves that disrupt youth's sense of stability
3. **Transition Planning**: Develop comprehensive housing transition plans for aging-out youth

### For Mental Health
1. **Early Intervention**: Implement preventive mental health services for youth experiencing recent negative life events
2. **Trauma-Informed Care**: Provide trauma-informed approaches for youth with depression and anxiety
3. **Continuity of Care**: Ensure mental health services continue during placement transitions

### For Supportive Adult Relationships
1. **Family Strengthening**: Build upon existing family strengths and improve family functioning
2. **Natural Support Development**: Actively work to identify and develop natural support systems
3. **Relationship Permanence**: Focus on creating stable, long-term relationships for youth

---

## Data Limitations

1. **Temporal Scope**: Data represents a specific time period (FY23-25)
2. **Assessment Timing**: CANS assessments may not capture real-time changes in youth circumstances
3. **Geographic Coverage**: While comprehensive, some rural counties may have limited representation
4. **Intervention Outcomes**: Limited data on the effectiveness of interventions and services
